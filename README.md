## Overview

THREADRUNNER is a counter-unmanned aerial system (C-UAS) doctrine and architecture based on three original doctrinal contributions. It addresses a documented and worsening strategic problem: the US defense enterprise is organized around the wrong question when it comes to drone threats.

The wrong question: *how do we build a more capable, more expensive system to defeat cheap adversarial drones?*

The right question: *how do we build a $10 system that makes a $1,000,000 defense platform economically undefendable?*

---

## Doctrinal Contributions

### 1. The Inverse Architecture Principle
*Blake Clark, 2026 — Original Doctrine*

Technology effectiveness increases with proximity to target, not with centralized command distance or compute investment. Centralizing intelligence away from the point of action is not a feature — it is a vulnerability that scales adversely with operational complexity.

### 2. Cost-Exchange Asymmetry
*Blake Clark, 2026 — Original Doctrine*

The critical threat posed by cheap adversarial drone swarms is not technological capability parity — it is economic unsustainability. Any C-UAS solution that fails to achieve a cost exchange ratio at or below 1:1 against the attacker's per-unit cost, at swarm densities of hundreds to thousands of units, is a delay, not a defense.

### 3. The Parasitic Intercept Paradigm
*Blake Clark, 2026 — Original Doctrine*

Rather than shooting down or jamming a drone, THREADRUNNER micro-munitions attach to the target and use its own flight energy to deliver payload. The target carries the weapon to itself. The attacker's asset becomes the defender's delivery vehicle.

## Architecture

THREADRUNNER has three integrated layers:

- **The Thread** — lightweight autonomous micro-munition designed for physical attachment to target airframe, delivering a small neutralization payload (entanglement, motor seizure, thermite core). Engineered for minimum viable cost and replaceability, not durability.

- **The Runner Network** — proximity-weighted mesh of distributed sensor and guidance nodes. No command node. Intelligence is distributed across the mesh, weighted by physical proximity to the threat. The brain is the topology. There is no head to cut off.

- **The Swarm Brain** — coordination layer implementing the Inverse Architecture Principle: as Threads converge on a target, their local sensor data propagates across the mesh, increasing targeting precision for the entire swarm in real time. The swarm gets smarter as it gets closer.

---

## Why It Breaks Current Doctrine

Existing C-UAS targeting assumes a command hierarchy with a head that can be neutralized. THREADRUNNER has no head. Destroy 90% of the Runner nodes and the remaining 10% continue operating the same protocol with degraded coverage but unchanged doctrine.

Existing countermeasures — jamming, EMP, kinetic neutralization — assume a cost exchange that favors the defender. THREADRUNNER inverts that assumption by design: at $25/Thread against a $500 adversary drone, the defender can deploy 20 Threads per drone and still achieve favorable cost exchange.
